Visual identity & style guide

Design system

The identity uses warm neutral surfaces with a teal primary accent and gold support tone, giving the site a technical but welcoming event feel rather than a generic template look.

Brand direction

The visual language is built around clarity, youth robotics energy, and competition-day usability. It is suitable for schedules, venue instructions, league explainers, and forms without redesigning each section.

Style rules

  • Display font: Cabinet Grotesk for strong section titles and event branding.
  • Body font: Satoshi for readable paragraphs, labels, and forms.
  • Rounded cards, restrained shadows, and editorial spacing for clarity.
  • Accent colors are used for actions and league distinction, not decoration overload.
  • Dark mode is built in and accessible from the header.

League pages

Competition leagues

RoboCupJunior features Soccer, Rescue, and OnStage, which makes these three sections the right foundation for your league-page work.

S

Soccer

Autonomous robots compete on the field by tracking the ball, coordinating movement, and playing structured matches as a team.

  • Good for rules, match format, field notes, and bracket links.
  • Add categories, team limits, and technical inspection notes.
  • Use a CTA like “View soccer handbook”.
R

Rescue

Rescue challenges robots to navigate line or disaster-style tasks while identifying and assisting victims through increasingly realistic scenarios.

  • Best for obstacle notes, scoring references, and arena guidance.
  • Add line, maze, or local sub-league breakdowns if needed.
  • Use a CTA like “See rescue rules”.
O

OnStage

OnStage focuses on creative robot performances and judging around presentation, technical demonstration, and overall audience impact.

  • Great for performance order, judging criteria, and media needs.
  • Add stage dimensions, sound check notes, and upload deadlines.
  • Use a CTA like “Prepare your performance”.
